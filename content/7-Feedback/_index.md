> This is my space to reflect honestly on the experience of participating in the First Cloud AI Journey program. I hope these notes are useful for the FCAJ team in making the program even better.

### Overall Evaluation

**1. Working Environment**  
The first thing that struck me was how open and approachable everyone was. No one hesitated to help when I got stuck — even outside working hours. The workspace was clean and low-distraction, which made it much easier to focus compared to studying at home. One thing that could add even more value: small informal gatherings to help members get to know each other better beyond just the work context.

**2. Support from the Admin Team**  
The admin team was thorough with technical guidance and, more importantly, created an environment where asking questions felt safe. What I appreciated most was that they rarely handed out direct answers — instead they nudged me to reason through problems myself. That approach made the knowledge actually stick rather than just getting me past the immediate roadblock.

**3. Relevance of the Training Program to Academic Major**  
The program filled in gaps that university coursework hasn't covered yet. Networking and database theory are taught in school, but getting to actually build and run things on AWS — with real infrastructure, not simulations — is something I didn't expect to have access to this year. That hands-on gap is where this program created the biggest difference.

**4. Learning & Skill Development Opportunities**  
Beyond technical skills, I picked up how to work within a structured process, how to use project management tools, and what professional communication actually looks like in practice. The external speaker sessions also gave me career perspectives I hadn't come across before, which helped me think more concretely about where I want to go.

**5. Learning Culture & Team Spirit**  
I didn't expect the team dynamic to be as strong as it was. When we hit tough system design problems or were pushing toward the final project deadline, nobody went off to work in isolation — people pulled together to discuss approaches and share knowledge. That collective energy accelerated my learning more than anything else.

**6. Internship Policies / Benefits**  
Flexible hours and access to real AWS resources for hands-on practice are the two things I valued most. Many internships put you in an observer role — here you actually build things. That distinction makes a substantial difference in what you walk away having learned.

### Additional Questions

- **What did you find most satisfying during your internship?**  
  Being directly involved in designing and building AWS architecture for a real project (the Flash Sale system) — not a practice exercise, but something with actual stakes. Paired with the genuine support from the admin team, it's something I didn't think I'd have access to when I first started.

- **What do you think the company should improve for future interns?**  
  A standardized reference document for architecture diagram conventions would be a meaningful addition. I spent a fair amount of time at the start figuring out how to present designs correctly. Having that resource from week one would let future interns skip that friction and focus more on the technical substance from the beginning.

- **If recommending to a friend, would you suggest they intern here? Why or why not?**  
  Yes, without hesitation. Not just because the technical content is strong, but because it's an environment where I genuinely felt myself growing — in how I think and in how I work. That kind of environment is harder to find than most people realize.
